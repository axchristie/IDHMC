"Spatial Humanities," as a field of research, encompasses at once:


  • Representations of space
  • Spatial technologies
  • Research spaces and infrastructures


How can such a spatial humanities help us think productively across research subjects and research environments?


Can we map subject/environment models of space across literary pasts and disciplinary presents? Should we?
